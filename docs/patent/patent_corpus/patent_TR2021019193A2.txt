TR TR TR TR TRDESCRIPTION Early-Onset Systemic Lupus Erythematosus Gene Panel Technical Field The invention relates to early-onset systemic lupus erythematosus gene panel and the method related to it. Current State of the Art Discovery of new genes with common or rare variants in Systemic Lupus Erythematosus and elucidation of common molecular pathways with different gene mutations in monogenic lupus are important steps to better understand this complex disease and sensitive medicine. Patients with early-onset diseases are better candidates for these multifactorial studies. In the current technical field, whole exome sequencing and screening of all genes in patients are used for the disease in question. However, this method requires high economic cost and long time. Therefore, the routine use of the method in question is not widespread. In the patent and literature search conducted on the known status of the technique, an academic publication titled 'Diagnostic utility of a targeted next-generation sequencing gene panel in the clinical suspicion of systemic autoinflammatory diseases: a multi-center study' dated 2018 was identified. The aforementioned publication is a study on targeted Next Generation Sequencing (NGS) screening in patients with suspected sAlDs (Systemic autoinflammatory diseases) to determine the diagnostic benefit of genetic screening. In conclusion, due to the above-mentioned drawbacks and the inadequacy of existing solutions on the subject, it has been made necessary to make a development in the relevant technical field. Brief Description of the Invention The present invention aims to solve the above-mentioned negativities with the gene panel and the method related to it, which meets the above-mentioned requirements, eliminates all the disadvantages and brings some additional advantages. The main purpose of the invention is to develop a method that provides significant contributions to the early detection of pathogenic variations, diagnosis and treatment of the disease. Thanks to the invention, it is possible to screen patients quickly and effectively with low costs and to provide early diagnosis of the disease. The invention aims to screen Early Onset Systemic Lupus Erythematosus patients and detect pathogenic variations rapidly and effectively with the next generation sequencing gene panel consisting of 31 genes (C1QA, C1QB, C'IQC, CiR, C1S, C2, C3, TREX1, RNASEHZA, RNASEH2B, RNASEH2C, SAMHD1, ADAR, DNASE1, DNASE1L3, PRKCD, ACP5, SLCTAT, IFIH1, TMEM173, SHOC2) categorized under the umbrella of 'Monogenic Iupus'. The structural and characteristic features and all advantages of the invention will be understood more clearly thanks to the detailed explanation given below, and therefore the evaluation should be made by taking this detailed explanation into consideration. Detailed Description of the Invention In this detailed description, the preferred structures of the gene panel which is the subject of the invention are explained only for a better understanding of the subject. The invention is related to a method for early detection, diagnosis and treatment of pathogenic variations. In the method which is the subject of the invention, firstly, samples with DNA concentration of 50 ng/ul and above from patients are diluted in order to be measured with Qubit device. In the next process step, DNA amounts of diluted and undiluted samples are measured. Then, samples are diluted in a way suitable for working with 10 ng DNA. In this step, water and sample amounts are adjusted in predetermined ratios. After adjusting water and sample amounts, PCR process step is started. For this purpose, since there are two different Demand Primer Pool, 2 tubes are prepared for each patient. Two wells are created for each patient in the plate and the plates are loaded into the thermal cycle system in accordance with the predetermined temperature and time. After the PCR, the plates in question are preferably removed from -20 °C. The PCR products of the two patients are collected in a single well in the plates removed from -20 °C and FuPa enzyme is added to them. In order to bind barcode adapters to the amplicons amplified by PCR and phosphorylate the amplicons and make them a substrate for the ligase enzyme, partial cuts should be made from the ends of the amplicons with Ion AmpIiSeqTM FuPa reagent. As in the previous step, the plates are loaded into the thermal cycle system in accordance with the predetermined temperature and time. Barcodes, P1 Adapter, IonXpressBarcodeX (separate for each barcode) and distilled water are added. In the next step, PCR is applied for the third time and the plates are loaded into the thermal cycle system in accordance with the previously determined temperature and time. The samples removed from -20 °C are placed on the rack and Switch solution and DNA lyase enzyme are added to 48 wells (from IonAmpIseq Library Kit 2.0 kit). The plates are loaded into the thermal cycle system in accordance with the previously determined temperature and time. After the process, the plate is removed to - 20 °C. The next step is purification. AMPure XP Reagent is added to the wells of the plates removed from -20 °C (BeckmanCoulter) and the lids are closed and preferably waited at room temperature for 5 minutes. In the meantime, 70% ethanol (99% ethanol and distilled water) is prepared and preferably 5 minutes later, the plate is placed on the DynaMAG 96 magnet plate and preferably waited for 5 minutes. In this way, the beads and DNA are collected in one place. The supernatants are drawn off with a pipette and then ethanol is drawn on top of the pellet. As in the previous step, 70% ethanol is added to the pellet and the plate is moved on the magnetic plate, then ethanol is drawn. -20 c(The samples removed from the well are placed on a rack and Platinum PCR Super mix HIFi and AmplificationPrimerMix from the IonAmpIiseq Library Kit 2.0 kit are added to the wells. The plate is placed on the magnetplate and preferably waited for 5 minutes. At this stage, the supernatants are transferred to a new plate. The new plate is loaded into the thermal cycler with the previously determined temperature and time setting. Then, AMPure XP Reagent is added to the wells and preferably waited for 5 minutes at room temperature and then preferably on the magnetplate for 5 minutes. The supernatant is drawn and discarded. Ethanol attached to the beads in the DNA pellet is added, the plate is moved and the supernatant is drawn and discarded. Low TE is added to protect the DNA.
